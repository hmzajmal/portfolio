# Portfolio Tone

The voice of hamzajamal.design. Anchored by three words. Strict gate on
copy, naming, framing, and visual decisions.

## Anchors

1. **Senior**
2. **Mature**
3. **Lead-brand**

## Supporting vocabulary

Reinforces the same tone. Use these as the secondary palette when writing
captions, CTAs, descriptions, headlines.

- Confident (not arrogant)
- Understated (Rolex / Linear / Stripe energy)
- Authoritative (measured, not loud)
- Strategic (outcomes, not deliverables)
- Crafted (precise, not "polished")
- Considered (deliberate, not reactive)
- Restrained (less is more)
- Earned (work speaks first, claims second)
- Direct (no hedging, no fluff)
- Grounded (real numbers, real shipped products)

## Anti-keywords. Reject on sight.

- Cute / quirky / playful as the primary mode (occasional moments fine,
  primary mode no)
- Self-deprecating jokes ("Legend of vibe coding", "I burn tokens")
- Trendy phrasing ("vibing", "shipping bangers", "0 to 1", "pixel pushing")
- Process-heavy language ("ideation", "double diamond", "research
  synthesis")
- Buzzword stacking ("behavioral UX", "design thinking", "user-centric")
- Emoji noise
- "Hi I'm..." openings
- Apologetic language ("a bit of a designer", "trying to figure out")
- Excessive enthusiasm (multiple exclamation points, "amazing",
  "incredible")

## The two decision rules

1. Would this stay on the site of a Head of Design at Linear, Stripe,
   Figma, or Vercel? If no, cut or rewrite.
2. Does this say "I shipped this" or "look at me"? First is leadership,
   second is junior. Cut the second.

## How to use this doc

When considering a copy change, visual addition, or feature:

- **Keep**: fits the tone, no changes
- **Reject + alternate**: explain why it breaks the tone, propose a
  tighter version
- **Reject hard**: idea itself doesn't belong; explain why

Defend the call with a reason, not opinion.
